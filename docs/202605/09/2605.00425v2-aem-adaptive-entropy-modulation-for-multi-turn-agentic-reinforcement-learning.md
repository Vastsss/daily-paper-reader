## 摘要
强化学习 (RL) 显著提升了大语言模型 (LLM) 智能体与环境交互及解决多轮任务的能力。然而，有效的智能体强化学习仍面临挑战：仅基于结果的稀疏奖励在长交互轨迹中为单个步骤分配信用（credit assignment）时提供的指导有限。现有方法通常引入密集的中间监督，例如过程奖励模型或辅助自监督信号，这增加了监督和调优的复杂性，并可能限制跨任务和跨领域的泛化能力。我们提出了 AEM，一种无需监督的信用分配方法，它在强化学习训练过程中自适应地调制熵动力学，以改善探索与利用之间的权衡。我们的分析将熵动力学从 token 级别提升到响应（response）级别，使不确定性估计与 LLM 智能体的有效动作粒度保持一致，并降低了对 token 级别采样噪声的敏感性。我们进一步证明，自然梯度更新下的熵漂移受采样响应的优势（advantage）与其相对惊奇度（surprisal）之间相互作用的支配。受此结果启发，AEM 推导出一个实用的响应级不确定性代理，并利用它来重新缩放优势值，利用正负样本之间不断演变的平衡，实现从探索到利用的自然过渡。在 ALFWorld、WebShop 和 SWE-bench-Verified 上进行的广泛实验（模型规模从 1.5B 到 32B）表明，AEM 持续改进了强大的强化学习基线，在集成到最先进的软件工程强化学习训练框架中时，获得了 +1.4% 的提升。

## Abstract
Reinforcement learning (RL) has substantially improved the ability of large language model (LLM) agents to interact with environments and solve multi-turn tasks. However, effective agentic RL remains challenging: sparse outcome-only rewards provide limited guidance for assigning credit to individual steps within long interaction trajectories. Existing approaches often introduce dense intermediate supervision, such as process reward models or auxiliary self-supervised signals, which increases supervision and tuning complexity and may limit generalization across tasks and domains. We present AEM, a supervision-free credit assignment method that adaptively modulates entropy dynamics during RL training to improve the exploration-exploitation trade-off. Our analysis lifts entropy dynamics from the token level to the response level, aligning uncertainty estimation with the effective action granularity of LLM agents and reducing sensitivity to token-level sampling noise. We further show that entropy drift under natural-gradient updates is governed by the interaction between the sampled-response advantage and its relative surprisal. Motivated by this result, AEM derives a practical response-level uncertainty proxy and uses it to rescale advantages, leveraging the evolving balance between positive and negative samples to naturally transition from exploration to exploitation. Extensive experiments on ALFWorld, WebShop, and SWE-bench-Verified with models ranging from 1.5B to 32B demonstrate that AEM consistently improves strong RL baselines, including a +1.4\% gain when integrated into a state-of-the-art software-engineering RL training framework.
