# Event-Based Dynamic Programming for Pumped-Storage Hydropower Scheduling 

Bo Yang, Kai Pan, Member, IEEE, Mohammad Reza Hesamzadeh, Senior Member, IEEE 

Abstract —This paper studies the single-unit pumped-storage hydropower (PSH) plant scheduling problem with reservoir dynamics, generation and pumping limits, ramping constraints, start-up and shut-down costs, and minimum up/down-time requirements. A new event-based formulation is proposed in which an operating schedule is represented as a sequence of mode-specific events, with dispatch decisions within each event determined by linear programs. Based on this construction, the original time-indexed mixed-integer formulation is reformulated exactly as a deterministic dynamic program on an event network. The framework is modular and can be extended to incorpo-rate additional operating modes, such as hydraulic short-circuit operation, by introducing corresponding event modules without significantly changing the overall event-network structure. To obtain tractable solution methods, a finite-grid approximation of the event network is developed, leading to a linear programming formulation for the discretized model. In addition, an event-based branch-and-bound algorithm with linear program-based bounds is proposed for the continuous-state problem. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed event-based framework provides a computationally effective alternative to the conventional time-indexed formulation, while offering substantial modeling flexibil-ity for PSH scheduling problems. 

Index Terms —Pumped-storage hydropower, unit commitment, dynamic programming, event-based modeling, linear program-ming. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

# THE increasing penetration of variable renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power, has reshaped modern power systems. Their intermittency and uncertainty pose new challenges for maintaining system reliability and economic efficiency, thereby increasing the need for large-scale energy storage. Among available storage technologies, pumped-storage hydropower (PSH) remains the most mature and widely deployed technology worldwide [1]. It accounts for the vast majority of installed utility-scale long-duration energy storage capacity and offers high operational flexibility and relatively low operating costs [2]. These features make PSH particularly valuable in power systems with high renewable penetration [3]. PSH stores energy by pumping water to an upper reservoir during low-price or surplus-generation periods and generating electricity during high-price or high-demand periods. This flexibility enables PSH plants to perform energy arbitrage, provide ancillary services such as frequency regulation and reserves, and contribute to system reliability [4], [5]. Modern PSH technologies, including variable-speed pump-turbines and hydraulic short-circuit (HSC) configurations, further expand the operational flexibility and market participation of PSH units [6], [7]. Capturing these capabilities in market and

However, the very features that make PSH operationally valuable also make it challenging to model in a compu-tationally efficient way. Existing PSH scheduling formula-tions are typically time-indexed mixed-integer linear programs (MILPs). While such formulations are expressive, their binary operating decisions and intertemporal reservoir constraints make them difficult to incorporate into larger market-clearing, unit-commitment, and bilevel optimization models. Embed-ding PSH models in such settings often requires convex-hull reformulations, but these reformulations are usually com-plex and difficult to extend when new operating modes are introduced. In addition, representing operating costs along-side intertemporal reservoir dynamics may require additional assumptions to maintain tractability. These challenges moti-vate us to combine operational richness with computational tractability. In this paper, we develop an event-based formulation for deterministic single-unit PSH scheduling and commitment. In-stead of modeling the operation period by period, we represent a schedule as a sequence of operating events, where each event corresponds to a fixed operating mode maintained over a time interval. Discrete mode-transition decisions are modeled as actions in a dynamic program (DP), while continuous within-event dispatch decisions are determined by mode-specific linear programs (LPs). This decomposition separates switching decisions from continuous operating decisions and yields an exact reformulation of the original time-indexed model as a deterministic DP on an event network. A key feature of the proposed framework is its modularity. Because the event-network structure is decoupled from the within-event operating models, new operating modes, such as HSC operation, can be incorporated by adding new event modules without changing the overall network structure. Based on the event-based formulation, we develop a finite-grid approximation of the continuous reservoir state. The resulting discretized event network can be represented as a time-expanded directed acyclic graph (DAG) and reformulated as a single LP. This LP combines network-flow constraints for event selection with embedded within-event operating models on each arc, thereby avoiding explicit precomputation and assumptions on operating costs between boundary states. In addition, the LP formulation characterizes the convex hull of the corresponding discretized mixed-integer model, as the associated network-flow constraint matrix on the DAG is totally unimodular. We also develop an exact event-based branch-and-bound (B&B) algorithm for the continuous-state problem, in which

The operation and scheduling of PSH plants in electricity markets have been studied extensively in the literature [8]. Early studies focused mainly on deterministic scheduling and energy arbitrage models [9]. More recent work has expanded these models to incorporate participation in multiple market products, such as energy and reserve markets [7]. Related studies have also considered HSC operation in short-term PSH scheduling and dispatch [6], [10]. A related line of research focuses on improving the representation of PSH units, including configuration-based formulations and convex-hull-based approximations of PSH operating characteristics [11], [12]. At the same time, increasing attention has been devoted to uncertainty in PSH scheduling and market participation [13], [14]. Our paper contributes to the PSH literature by offering a new perspective on the formulation and solution of deterministic single-unit PSH scheduling problems. From a computational perspective, recent work has de-veloped convex-hull formulations, DP-based approaches, and convex-relaxation techniques to improve tractability for hydro and storage scheduling problems [15], [16]. Related ideas also appear in convex-hull-based reformulations in which a mixed-integer scheduling problem is first recast as a DP and then transformed into an LP [17], [18]. More broadly, these developments are connected to the literature on polyhedral descriptions of DP and network-based formulations [19], [20], as well as to polyhedral studies of unit commitment and ramping constraints [21]. Related network-based modeling ideas have also been explored in block-based state-expanded formulations in other scheduling contexts [22]. Our work is closest in spirit to this line of research, but differs in several important respects. In particular, existing DP-to-LP reformula-tions typically rely on a relatively direct LP transcription of a discrete-state DP. In contrast, the LP formulation in our paper arises from an event-based representation of PSH scheduling and is based on the event-network structure and the total unimodularity of the underlying DAG formulation. More broadly, our work relates to the literature on strong formulations for unit commitment and storage scheduling. Prior studies have developed efficient, strong, and compact MILP formulations for unit commitment [23], [24], [25], [26], and have highlighted the trade-off between formulation strength and compactness [27]. Related work on storage-integrated unit commitment further underscores the need for scalable methods with strong relaxations and provable bounds [28], [29]. Our paper contributes to this literature through an event-based reformulation that accommodates mode tran-sitions, reservoir dynamics, and modular operating modes. This section reformulates the single-unit PSH commitment model in §II as an event-based model. An event is defined as an activity during which the unit remains in a fixed operating mode over a time interval. For each event, the associated cost is determined by the operating decisions made within that interval. This reformulation embeds the discrete mode switching decisions of the time-indexed model into the event definition, while retaining the remaining continuous decisions as intra-event variables. By treating the overall operating schedule as a sequence of events, this perspective leads to a deterministic event-based DP.

An important computational advantage of the finite-grid LP approach is that the operating model is arc-separable. We also consider an extension with HSC operation; the corresponding numerical results are reported in Appendix I-D. VII. C ONCLUSION 

This paper develops an event-based framework for single-unit pumped-storage hydropower scheduling. The proposed formulation yields an exact DP reformulation of the original time-indexed model, a finite-grid LP for the discretized problem, and an exact event-based B&B method for the continuous-state problem. Numerical results show that the LP is exact on the discretized state space and scales favorably with the planning horizon, while the B&B method matches the benchmark MILP in the continuous-state setting. The modularity and the computational efficiency of the proposed approach, in particular the LP formulation, makes it particu-larly attractive for large-scale and market-level applications, where the PSH scheduling problem may need to be solved repeatedly or embedded within larger optimization models.
