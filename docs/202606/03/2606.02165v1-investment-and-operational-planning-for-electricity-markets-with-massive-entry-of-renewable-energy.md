## 摘要
在批发电力市场中，电力生产商和独立系统运营商（ISO）扮演着核心角色。ISO负责在满足供需平衡和容量约束的同时最小化生产成本。本文研究了一个连续时间问题，其中ISO寻求最小化电力网络中的运行与投资联合成本。该问题以运行和投资控制变量形式表述。我们分析了这些控制之间的层级关系，并利用所谓的“日前问题”找到最优运行的显式形式。这使得我们将投资问题重新表述为具有状态约束的随机控制问题。我们扩展了状态约束随机控制的结果以适应我们的设定。特别地，我们使用向内指向条件的一个版本，将问题的价值完全刻画为受约束HJB方程的唯一粘性解。然后，我们为容量-需求过程分配特定的动态，并讨论HJB刻画结果的假设如何导致规划的预算约束。最后，我们对一个类似于智利市场的三节点场景进行模拟。我们分析了短期、中期和长期规划情景，并讨论了如何向高比例可再生能源系统过渡。

## Abstract
In wholesale electricity markets, electricity producers and the independent system operator (ISO) play a central role. The ISO is responsible for minimizing production costs while satisfying supply-demand balance and capacity constraints. In this paper, we study a continuous-time problem in which the ISO seeks to minimize the joint cost of operation and investment in an electricity network. The problem is formulated in terms of operational and investment control variables. We analyze the hierarchy between these controls and use the so-called Day-Ahead Problem to find an explicit form of the optimal operation. This allows us to reformulate the investment problem as a stochastic control problem with state constraints. We extend the results of state-constrained stochastic control to fit our setting. In particular, we use a version of the Pointing Inward Condition to fully characterize the value of the problem as the unique viscosity solution of a constrained HJB equation. We then assign a specific dynamic to the capacity-demand process and discuss how the assumptions for the HJB characterization result in a budget constraint for the planning. Finally, we run simulations for a three-node setting that resembles the Chilean market. We analyze short-, medium-, and long-term planning scenarios and discuss how to transition toward a system with high penetration of renewable energy.
